# Dictionary of Deception

Translate common buzzwords into plain speech. Use this to spot when power hides behind friendly language.

| Term | What they say | What's really happening |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **"Regulatory certainty"** | "Businesses need stable rules." | Lobbyists want rules that freeze out newcomers. |
| **"Public-private partnership"** | "A collaboration for the public good." | Profit is privatized; risk and cost are socialized. |
| **"Intellectual property"** | "Protecting innovation." | Granting temporary monopolies that tax all future inventors. |
| **"Right-to-work"** | "Freedom from forced union membership." | Weakening worker bargaining while maintaining corporate hierarchies. |
| **"Smart city"** | "Tech-enabled convenience." | Data extraction and surveillance wrapped in glossy UI. |
| **"Infrastructure incentive package"** | "Job creation." | Taxpayer-funded gifts to corporations that may leave when the subsidy expires. |
| **"Gig economy flexibility"** | "Be your own boss!" | Shift costs and risk to workers without giving them ownership. |
| **"National security"** | "Protecting you from threats." | A broad pretext for surveillance, secrecy, and military spending that stabilizes global corporate supply chains. |
| **"Free trade agreement"** | "Lowering barriers between nations." | Thousands of pages of investor protections and intellectual property rules that entrench incumbents. |

> **Use the decoder.** When you hear these phrases, ask: who gains new privileges, who shoulders new risks, and who gets to opt out?
